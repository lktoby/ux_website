Why did you decide to transfer?

I only took the transfer exam for INIAD lol

When I was picking universities, these points about Toyo and INIAD caught my attention:

  • There are a lot of support as well as international communication opportunities within the university.
  • INIAD Graduates seem to go to good companies.
  • The Subject Group system seems interesting and allows me to study deeper into my field of interest.

At first I did not score very well in the EJU (entrance exam for international students) that I just entered somewhere that accepted me. I wanted to try one more time even after I got into somewhere else so I tried again and successfully got in.

Preparing for the transfer exam was hard, I was very glad that I made this decision and I feel grateful for it every day.

First, I reached out to INIAD office. Then I studied the past questions (Computer Science, Math).

Then I received an email about my exam results and attended an interview with some INIAD professors.

Japanese must've been hard, yeah?

Very much yes.

Though, I was blessed to have grown up in a Canto-English bilingual environment, that I found familiarity in Knaji (Chinese characters) like Chinese learners, as well as Katakana loan words like English-speaking learners.

Polite language still hurts my brain though.

I do plan to work in Japan after I graduate, but receving my salary in Japanese yen does make me sad a little bit. :(

I use Japanese to study, take notes, as well as to communicate with other local students.

However, as I'm studying something technical and type code in English (as programming languages are closer to English), I just search for English sources if I ever encounter a Japanese phrase that I have no idea about.

There are English versions for most lecture notes, but I mostly read the Japanese versions. I only use the English ones for the Math subjects.
